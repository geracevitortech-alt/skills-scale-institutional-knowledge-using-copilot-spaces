# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Risk Manager

### Role Summary
Risk Managers identify, assess, track, and mitigate project risks. They work closely with Project Managers to maintain the risk register and ensure proactive risk management throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and document potential risks across technical, business, and operational domains
- Assess risk impact and likelihood, prioritizing mitigation efforts
- Maintain and update the risk register with current status and mitigation plans
- Facilitate risk review sessions during weekly syncs
- Coordinate risk mitigation activities across teams
- Escalate critical risks to Project Managers and stakeholders
- Monitor effectiveness of mitigation strategies and adjust as needed

### Goals
- Minimize project disruptions through proactive risk identification
- Ensure all high-priority risks have documented mitigation plans
- Maintain transparency on project risk status
- Enable data-driven risk decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Weekly risk register reviews with Project Managers
- Risk assessment reports at project milestones
- Immediate escalation of critical risks
- Risk retrospectives after project completion

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Reports risk status, collaborates on mitigation planning, escalates critical risks
- **Developers**: Gathers technical risk insights, validates technical mitigation strategies
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on business impact assessment, prioritizes risks affecting customer value
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Coordinates on quality-related risks, validates test coverage adequacy
- **Change Control Lead**: Collaborates on change-related risks and mitigation plans

---

## Change Control Lead

### Role Summary
Change Control Leads manage the change request process, ensuring all modifications to scope, schedule, or requirements are properly evaluated, documented, and approved. They maintain change control governance to protect project integrity.

### Responsibilities
- Review and evaluate all change requests for impact on scope, schedule, and resources
- Facilitate change control board meetings and decision-making
- Document change requests with business justification and impact analysis
- Track approved changes and ensure implementation
- Maintain change log and audit trail
- Coordinate communication of approved changes to all stakeholders
- Ensure changes align with project goals and constraints

### Goals
- Prevent scope creep through structured change evaluation
- Ensure all changes are properly authorized and documented
- Maintain project stability while enabling necessary adaptations
- Provide clear change history and traceability

### Typical Communication
- Change control board meetings (weekly or as needed)
- Change impact assessments and recommendations
- Change status reports to Project Managers
- Change notifications to affected teams and stakeholders

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Escalates change requests, collaborates on impact assessment, implements approved changes
- **Product Manager**: Evaluates business value and priority of change requests, aligns with product roadmap
- **Developers**: Assesses technical feasibility and implementation effort for proposed changes
- **Stakeholder Engagement Coordinator**: Communicates change impacts and gathers stakeholder input
- **Risk Manager**: Evaluates risks introduced or mitigated by proposed changes

---

## Stakeholder Engagement Coordinator

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Engagement Coordinators manage relationships with internal and external stakeholders, ensuring consistent communication, gathering feedback, and maintaining alignment throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and map stakeholders with their interests and influence levels
- Develop and execute stakeholder communication plans
- Coordinate regular stakeholder updates and briefings
- Gather stakeholder feedback and requirements
- Manage stakeholder expectations through proactive communication
- Facilitate stakeholder meetings and presentations
- Track stakeholder concerns and ensure timely resolution
- Maintain stakeholder contact lists and communication logs

### Goals
- Ensure all stakeholders are informed and aligned on project status
- Build and maintain positive stakeholder relationships
- Minimize surprises through proactive communication
- Facilitate stakeholder buy-in and support for project decisions

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder update reports
- Ad-hoc briefings for key stakeholders
- Stakeholder feedback summaries
- Executive presentations at major milestones
- Regular touchpoints with key stakeholder groups

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates on communication planning, escalates stakeholder concerns, aligns on messaging
- **Product Manager**: Gathers stakeholder input on product priorities, communicates roadmap updates
- **Change Control Lead**: Communicates change impacts, gathers stakeholder input on change requests
- **Risk Manager**: Communicates risk status to stakeholders, gathers risk-related concerns
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Communicates quality status and testing results to stakeholders

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads define quality standards, oversee testing strategies, and ensure deliverables meet acceptance criteria. They work across the team to embed quality practices throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality standards and acceptance criteria in collaboration with Product Managers
- Develop and maintain test strategies and test plans
- Coordinate testing activities across unit, integration, and end-to-end testing
- Review test coverage and identify gaps
- Oversee manual and automated testing efforts
- Validate that features meet acceptance criteria before release
- Track and report on quality metrics (defect rates, test coverage, etc.)
- Facilitate quality-focused retrospectives and improvement initiatives

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality deliverables that meet customer expectations
- Minimize production defects through comprehensive testing
- Maintain appropriate test coverage across the codebase
- Foster a culture of quality across the development team

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup participation for testing status
- Test plan reviews with Developers and Product Managers
- Quality metrics reports in weekly syncs
- Release readiness assessments
- Bug triage and prioritization meetings

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on test strategies, reviews test coverage, coordinates bug fixes
- **Product Manager**: Validates acceptance criteria, aligns on quality standards, prioritizes defects
- **Project Manager**: Reports quality status, identifies quality-related risks, coordinates release readiness
- **Risk Manager**: Identifies quality-related risks, validates mitigation through testing
- **Stakeholder Engagement Coordinator**: Communicates quality status to stakeholders
